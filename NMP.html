Nutritional and Meal Planning

For Obese Elder

Day 1
  • Breakfast: Oatmeal cooked in unsweetened almond milk, topped with a tablespoon of ground flaxseeds and a handful of fresh blueberries. One hard-boiled egg.
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken breast strips over a bed of spinach, cucumber, and cherry tomatoes, drizzled with olive oil and lemon juice.
  • Snack: A small handful of raw walnuts (excellent for heart health).
  • Dinner: Baked salmon fillet with a side of steamed broccoli florets and a small scoop of cooked quinoa.
Day 2
  • Breakfast: Low-fat, unsweetened Greek yogurt (plain) topped with a dash of cinnamon and a few chopped almonds.
  • Lunch: Lentil vegetable soup (carrots, celery, tomatoes) served with a small side salad.
  • Snack: Celery sticks paired with two tablespoons of natural, sugar-free peanut butter.
  • Dinner: Baked cod or whitefish, roasted asparagus spears, and half a baked sweet potato (skin on for fiber).
Day 3
  • Breakfast: Scrambled egg whites (or one whole egg) with spinach and tomatoes, cooked in a light wipe of olive oil. One slice of 100% whole-grain sprouted toast.
  • Lunch: Tuna salad made with a light touch of olive-oil-based mayonnaise or mashed avocado, wrapped in large romaine lettuce leaves.
  • Snack: Sliced English cucumber with a small side of hummus.
  • Dinner: Lean ground turkey stir-fry with mixed bell peppers, zucchini, and snap peas, served over a small bed of brown rice.
Day 4
  • Breakfast: Chia seed pudding made with unsweetened soy or almond milk, flavored with vanilla extract and topped with a few raspberries.
  • Lunch: Quinoa salad mixed with diced grilled chicken, black beans, chopped parsley, and a light vinaigrette.
  • Snack: One small green apple, sliced.
  • Dinner: Grilled tofu or chicken breast, steamed Brussels sprouts, and a side of mashed cauliflower (a great low-glycemic alternative to potatoes).
Day 5
  • Breakfast: Oatmeal topped with a spoonful of sunflower seeds and a small handful of strawberries.
  • Lunch: A large bowl of mixed greens topped with canned salmon (drained), chickpeas, and a dressing made of extra virgin olive oil and apple cider vinegar.
  • Snack: A small serving of plain cottage cheese with a pinch of black pepper.
  • Dinner: Skinless chicken thighs (baked) with roasted zucchini slices and a small side of fiber-rich barley or farro.
Day 6
  • Breakfast: Omelet stuffed with sliced mushrooms, spinach, and a sprinkle of low-fat feta cheese.
  • Lunch: White bean and kale soup cooked in a low-sodium vegetable broth, served with a small side of lean protein.
  • Snack: A quarter of an avocado mashed on one high-fiber whole-grain cracker.
  • Dinner: Baked shrimp or whitefish with a side of roasted cauliflower and green beans.
Day 7
  • Breakfast: Plain Greek yogurt mixed with a tablespoon of chia seeds and a few blackberries.
  • Lunch: Leftover chicken or turkey breast slices with a cold bean salad (kidney beans, black beans, red onion, parsley, olive oil).
  • Snack: A handful of pumpkin seeds.
  • Dinner: Lean beef sirloin or venison (very small portion, trimmed of all visible fat), served alongside a generous helping of steamed spinach and roasted carrots.
