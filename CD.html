CHRONIC DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Chronic diseases are long-term medical conditions that usually develop slowly and may last for many years or throughout a person’s lifetime. These illnesses often require continuous medical attention, lifestyle changes, and regular monitoring to prevent complications and improve quality of life.

TIPS, GUIDES & ADVICE
  • Eat balanced meals with vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and lean protein.
  • Reduce salty, sugary, and oily foods.
  • Drink enough water daily.
  • Exercise regularly such as walking, stretching, or light home workouts for at least 30 minutes.
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol intake.
  • Maintain a healthy body weight.

Chronic disease management improves wellness and prevents complications by:

  • Regular medical checkups
  • Proper medication management
  • Healthy eating habits
  • Physical activity and exercise
  • Stress management
  • Patient education and self-care

Common chronic diseases include:

  • Diabetes
  • Hypertension
  • Heart Disease
  • Arthritis
  • Asthma
  • Chronic Kidney Disease
